TEAMINER
Policies and Procedures

SECTION 1 - CODE OF ETHICS

Teaminer has made a commitment to provide blockchain products to its Independent Members. In turn, the company expects Teaminer Independent Members to reflect that image in their relationships with Customers and fellow Independent Members. As a Teaminer Independent Member you are expected to operate your business according to the highest standards of integrity and fair practice in your role as a Teaminer Independent Member. Failure to comply with the Code of Ethics can result in your termination as a Teaminer Independent Member. The Code of Ethics, therefore, states: As an Independent Member:

• I will conduct my business in an honest, ethical manner at all times.
• I will make no representations about the benefits of being an Independent Member with Teaminer other than those contained in officially-approved corporate literature and videos.
• I will provide support and encouragement to my customers to ensure that their experience with Teaminer is a successful one.
• I will motivate and actively work with Independent Members of my downline organization to help them build their Teaminer business. I understand that that this support is critical to each Independent Member’s success with Teaminer.
• I will refrain from exaggerating my personal income or the income potential in general and will stress to Independent Member prospects the level of effort and commitment required to succeed in the business.
• I will not abuse the goodwill of my association with Teaminer to further or promote other business interests (particularly those which may be competitive to Teaminer) without the prior written consent of Teaminer.
• I will not make disparaging remarks about other products, services, Independent Members, or companies; likewise, I will not willfully denigrate the activities or personalities of fellow Teaminer Independent Members.
• I will abide by all of the Policies and Procedures of Teaminer as included herein, or as may be amended from time to time.
• I will not make any payment(s) or promise to pay any prospective or existing Independent Member in return for such Independent Member’s enrollment, continued enrollment, or team building or recruiting activities with Teaminer.
• I will strive to sell and promote the products of Teaminer in a professional manner to end user retail customers.

(c) the enrollment or attempted enrollment of non-existent individuals as Independent Members or merchants;
(d) the use of a credit card by or on behalf of an Independent Member or merchant when the Independent Member or customer is not the account holder of such credit card;
(e) purchasing Teaminer products on behalf of another Independent Member, or under another Independent Member’s ID number, to qualify for commissions or bonuses.

In their enthusiasm to enroll prospective Independent Members, some Independent Members are occasionally tempted to make income claims or earnings representations to demonstrate the inherent power of network marketing. Moreover, the Federal Trade Commission and all states have laws or regulations that regulate or even prohibit certain types of income claims and testimonials made by persons engaged in network marketing. While Independent Members may believe it beneficial to provide copies of checks, or to disclose the earnings of themselves or others, such approaches have legal consequences that can negatively impact Teaminer as well as the Independent Member making the claim unless appropriate disclosures required by law are also made contemporaneously with the income claim or earnings representation. Because Independent Members do not have the data necessary to comply with the legal requirements for making income claims, an Independent Member may NOT make income projections, income claims or disclose his or her Teaminer income (including the showing of checks, copies of checks, bank statements or tax records).

4.27 - Telemarketing
The Federal Trade Commission and the Federal Communications Commission each have laws that restrict telemarketing practices.

Both federal agencies (as well as a number of states) have “do not call” regulations as part of their telemarketing laws. While you may not consider yourself a “telemarketer” in the traditional sense of the word, these regulations broadly define the term “telemarketer” and “telemarketing” so that your inadvertent action of calling someone whose telephone number is listed on the federal “do not call” registry could cause you to violate the law. Moreover, these regulations must not be taken lightly, as they carry significant penalties (up to $11,000.00 per violation).

Therefore, Independent Members must not engage in telemarketing relative to the operation of their Teaminer businesses. The term “telemarketing” means the placing of one or more telephone calls to an individual or entity to induce the purchase of a Teaminer product or service, or to recruit them for the Teaminer opportunity. “Cold calls” made to prospective customers or Independent Members that promote either Teaminer’s products or services or the Teaminer opportunity constitute telemarketing and are prohibited. However, a telephone call(s) placed to a prospective customer or Independent Member (a “prospect”) is permissible under the following situations:

• If the Independent Member has an established business relationship with the prospect. An “established business relationship” is a relationship between an Independent Member and a prospect based on the prospect’s purchase, rental or lease of goods or services from the Independent Member, or a financial transaction between the prospect and the Independent Member, within the 18 months immediately preceding the date of a telephone call to induce the prospect’s purchase of a product or service.
• The prospect’s personal inquiry or application regarding a product or service offered by the Independent Member within the 3 months immediately preceding the date of such a call.
• If the Independent Member receives written and signed permission from the prospect authorizing the Independent Member to call. The authorization must specify the telephone number(s) that the Independent Member is authorized to call.
• You may call family members, personal friends and acquaintances. An “acquaintance” is someone with whom you have at least a recent first-hand relationship (i.e., you have recently personally met him or her). Bear in mind, however, that if you make a habit of “card collecting” with everyone you meet and subsequently calling them, the FTC may consider this a form of telemarketing that is not subject to this exemption. Thus, if you engage in calling “acquaintances,” you must make such calls on an occasional basis only and not make this a routine practice.

In addition, Independent Members shall not use automatic telephone dialing systems relative to the operation of their Teaminer businesses. The term “automatic telephone dialing system” means equipment which has the capacity to (a) store or produce telephone numbers to be called using a random or sequential number generator and (b) to dial such numbers.

Teaminer wants to provide its Independent Members with the best products, compensation plan and service in the industry. Accordingly, we value your constructive criticisms and comments. All such comments should be submitted in writing to the Teaminer corporate offices. While Teaminer welcomes constructive input, negative comments and remarks made in the field by Independent Members about the Company, its products or Compensation Plan serve no purpose other than to sour the enthusiasm of other Teaminer Independent Members. For this reason, and to set the proper example for their Downline, Independent Members must not disparage, demean or make negative remarks about Teaminer, other Teaminer Independent Members, Teaminer’s services, the Compensation Plan or Teaminer’s directors, officers or employees.

5.4 - Providing Documentation to Applicants
Independent Members must provide the most current version of the Policies and Procedures and the Compensation Plan to individuals whom they are sponsoring to become Independent Members before the applicant signs an Independent Member Agreement. Additional copies of Policies and Procedures can be found on the Teaminer website at healerteam.net, or in your business center under the forms section.

5.5 - Reporting Policy Violations
Independent Members observing a policy violation by another Independent Member should submit a written report of the violation directly to the attention of the Teaminer Compliance Department. Details of the incident(s), such as dates, number of occurrences, persons involved and any supporting documentation, should be included in the report.

SECTION 6 - REQUIREMENTS

6.1 - Product Sales
The Teaminer Compensation Plan is based upon the sale of Teaminer products and services to end user consumers. Independent Members must fulfill personal and Downline organization sales requirements (as well as meet other responsibilities set forth in the Agreement) to be eligible for bonuses, commissions and advancement to higher levels of achievement.

6.2 – Retail Sales
Teaminer wants to ensure that prices for its products and services are not destabilized when sold through a retailing environment. Therefore, Products sold in a retail environment will be subject to a minimum advertised retail price. The minimum advertised price of Teaminer’s products sold in a retail environment (such as a grocery store, eBay, convenience store, market, etc.) is listed on the Teaminer website. Any Independent Member who knowingly fails to honor the minimum price set by Teaminer for its products and services will be subject to termination.

Sales of Teaminer products through on-line auction sites, such as eBay are prohibited, unless such sale is done using a fixed price which is not below the minimum advertised retail price.

6.3 - Territory Restrictions
There are no exclusive territories granted to anyone. No franchise fees are required.

7.1 - Bonus and Commission Qualifications
An Independent Member must be active and in compliance with the Agreement and these policies to qualify for bonuses and commissions. So long as an Independent Member complies with the terms of the Agreement and these policies, Teaminer shall pay commissions to such Independent Member in accordance with the Compensation Plan. The minimum amount for which Teaminer will issue a commission payment is $25.00.

7.2 - Commission Payments and Promotions
7.2.1 - Payments, Calculations, and Bonuses
Commissions will be mailed out in accordance with the Compensation Plan. Commissions will be calculated according to the level for which an Independent Member actually satisfied all of the requirements according to the Compensation Plan rather than the highest rank or title achieved. Commission reports will be provided to Independent Members on-line, via web access.
7.2.2 - Promotions
Promotions are determined based on business organization and sales activity for each applicable period.

7.3 - Adjustment to Bonuses and Commissions

Independent Members receive bonuses and commissions based on the actual enrollment for services to merchants. When a service is cancelled and refund is authorized by the Company, the bonuses and commissions attributable to the refunded service(s) will be deducted in the month in which the refund is given, and continuing every pay period thereafter until the commission is recovered from the Independent Members who received bonuses and commissions on the sales of the refunded service(s).

SECTION 10 - INACTIVITY AND CANCELLATION

10.1 - Effect of Cancellation
So long as an Independent Member remains active and complies with the terms of the Independent Member Agreement and these Policies and Procedures, Teaminer shall pay commissions to such Independent Member in accordance with the Compensation Plan. An Independent Member’s bonuses and commissions constitute the entire consideration for the Independent Member’s efforts in generating sales and all activities related to generating sales (including building a Downline Organization). Following an Independent Member’s termination for inactivity, or voluntary or involuntary termination of his or her Independent Member Agreement (all of these methods are collectively referred to as “termination”), the former Independent Member shall have no right, title, claim or interest to the marketing organization that he or she operated, or any commission or bonus from the sales generated by the organization. An Independent Member whose business is terminated will lose all rights as an Independent Member . This includes the right to sell Teaminer products and services and the right to receive future commissions, bonuses or other income resulting from the sales and other activities of the Independent Member’s former Downline sales organization. In the event of termination, Independent Members agree to waive all rights they may have, including but not limited to property rights, to their former Downline organization and to any bonuses, commissions or other remuneration derived from the sales and other activities of his or her former Downline organization.

Following an Independent Member’s termination of his or her Independent Member Agreement, the former Independent Member shall not hold himself or herself out as a Teaminer Independent Member. An Independent Member whose Independent Member Agreement is terminated shall receive commissions and bonuses only for the last full pay period he or she was active prior to cancellation (less any amounts withheld during an investigation preceding an involuntary termination).

10.2 - Involuntary Termination
An Independent Member’s violation of any of the terms of the Agreement, including any amendments that may be made by Teaminer in its sole discretion, may result in any of the sanctions listed in Section 9.1, including the involuntary termination of his or her Independent Member Agreement. Cancellation shall be effective on the date on which written notice is mailed, faxed or delivered to an express courier to the Independent Member’s last known address (or fax number), or to his or her attorney, or when the Independent Member receives actual notice of termination, whichever occurs first.

10.3 - Voluntary Termination
An Independent Member has a right to cancel, at any time, regardless of reason. Termination must be submitted in writing to the Company at its principal business address. The written notice must include the Independent Member’s signature, printed name, address and Independent Member ID number. Independent Members who have resigned may re-apply to become an Independent Member with Teaminer after 6 months. An Independent Member’s position is subject to termination due to inactivity (i.e., merchant enrollments, no commissions, no sponsoring; and no attendance at any Teaminer functions, participation in any other form of Independent Member activity, or operation of any other Teaminer business) after being inactive for 6 full calendar months.

10.4 - Non-Renewal
An Independent Member may also voluntarily cancel his or her Independent Member Agreement by failing to maintain the Agreement annually. The Company may also elect not to renew an Independent Member’s Agreement.

10.5- Complete Agreement
These Policies and Procedures, any and all modifications made by the Company, along with the Terms and Conditions and the Compensation Plan make up the entire agreement between Independent Member and Company.
